The idea of insects as a primary food source for humanity, also known as entomophagy, has gained attention due to several reasons
Sustainability: Insects are incredibly efficient at converting feed into protein. They often require fewer resources (such as water, land, and feed) compared to traditional livestock like cows, pigs, or chickens. They emit fewer greenhouse gases and can be reared using organic waste.
Nutritional Value: Many insects are rich in protein, healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals. For example, crickets, mealworms, and grasshoppers are high in protein and contain essential amino acids, making them a nutritious food source.
Cultural Acceptance: Insects are already consumed as a regular part of diets in various cultures around the world. Over 2 billion people globally eat insects as part of their traditional diets.
Environmental Impact: With the growing concern about the environmental impact of meat production, exploring alternative protein sources like insects becomes crucial to mitigate the strain on resources and reduce environmental degradation.
